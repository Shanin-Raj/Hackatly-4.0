Get Ready for Hackatly 4.0

Hackatly 4.0 is a national hackathon conducted by the IEDC unit of Thalassery Engineering College. We are taking a different route from previous hackathons this time, expecting many teams to participate in this year's event.

Hackatly 4.0 Phases

Hackatly 4.0 is conducting the hackathon in two distinct phases to ensure the best ideas come to life.

Phase I – Ideation Round

Focuses on generating innovative ideas based on the given themes. Submissions will be evaluated, and the most promising concepts will be shortlisted for Phase 2.

Phase II – Implementation

Shortlisted teams will develop their ideas into functional solutions and present them to a panel of industry experts. Projects evaluated on innovation, execution, and impact.

About Us

A flagship initiative of Kerala Startup Mission to promote innovation and entrepreneurship among the student and academic fraternity in the educational institutions in the State of Kerala. It is considered an umbrella program that would play an instrumental role in fostering innovation culture in Academic institutions.


IEDC CETLY, formed in the year 2015, is a centre supported by the Kerala Startup mission encouraging students to think creatively and generate innovative ideas. The centre aims to nurture a mindset to think creatively and innovate to develop useful products. IEDC CE-TLY serves as a helping hand among the students within the campus to figure out the best ideas and provide them with all the requirements, thereby resulting in finding the best upcoming entrepreneurs.

Our Legacy

IEDC unit of Thalassery Engineering college has been conducting hackatly for past few years. We have been successful in encouraging great ideas from great minds.

F.A.Qs

1. Where is it?

Hackatly will be taking place at College of Engineering Thalassery, Eranholi P.O., Thalassery-670107.

2. Who can attend?

Be a beginner or a pro, anyone of the age 18 or above can attend Hackatly. All are welcomed and encouraged to join us.

3. How much will it cost?

Even though most of the resources will be free, there is a registration fee of 500 for each participant entering the third phase.

4. Do I need to know coding?

Coding experience is not at all necessary. Non-STEM majors, first-time hackers, and beginners are welcomed and encouraged to join us. We will have mentors to guide you.

5. When will the registration close?

The registration begins from 19 October 2024 and will be closed by 28 October 2024. So hurry up!!!!!

6. What should I bring?

Bring your college ID card, laptop, and all other necessary items you need to use.

7. Do I need a team?

If you are planning to compete for the prize, you may be in a team of 2-4 people. The members of your team can also be from different colleges.

8. How do I register?

Click on the registration button, fill out the form (make sure that all the details are entered correctly) and we will get you ready to hack!
